Finding a technology partner isn't like hiring a contractor. A good partner doesn't just execute — they understand your business, challenge your assumptions, and guide you toward better outcomes.

## Beyond Technical Expertise

Technical skill is table stakes. What separates a good partner from a great one:

### They Ask Questions Before Proposing Solutions

A partner who jumps straight to solutions hasn't taken the time to understand your problem. Look for someone who asks "why" before "how."

### They Understand Business, Not Just Code

Technology decisions have business implications. Your partner should speak both languages fluently.

### They Push Back When Necessary

A partner who agrees with everything isn't adding value. The best partners will tell you when your approach needs rethinking.

### They Plan for After Launch

Building software is only half the story. A good partner thinks about maintenance, support, training, and evolution from the start.

## Red Flags

- Proposals that focus on technology choices before understanding requirements
- Fixed timelines without discovery phase
- No mention of process analysis or user research
- One-size-fits-all solutions
- No plan for knowledge transfer

## What We Believe
